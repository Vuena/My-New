How is Life Insurance Calculated?

Life insurance premiums are determined by a variety of risk factors that insurers use to estimate life expectancy. The most critical factors include:

  • Age: Generally, premiums increase as you age. Locking in a rate when you are younger can save you significantly over the life of the policy.
  • Health: Your medical history, BMI, and current health status play a major role. "Preferred" or "Excellent" health ratings get the best rates.
  • Habits: Smoking or using tobacco products can double or triple your premiums compared to non-smokers.
  • Gender: Statistically, women tend to live longer than men, often resulting in slightly lower premiums for the same coverage.

Term vs. Whole Life Insurance: A Quick Guide

Choosing between Term and Whole Life insurance depends on your financial goals and needs.

Term Life

Provides coverage for a specific period (e.g., 10, 20, or 30 years). It is generally more affordable and offers high coverage amounts for a lower monthly cost. Ideal for income replacement during working years.

Whole Life

Provides permanent coverage for your entire life as long as premiums are paid. It often includes a cash value component that grows over time. Premiums are significantly higher but fixed for life.

Tips to Lower Your Monthly Premiums

Looking to get the best rate possible? Consider these tips:

  • Quit Smoking: Being smoke-free for at least 12 months can qualify you for non-smoker rates.
  • Improve Your Health: Lowering cholesterol, blood pressure, and maintaining a healthy weight can improve your health class rating.
  • Buy Early: Rates are based on your age at application. Waiting just one year can increase your base rate by 4-8%.
  • Pay Annually: Many insurers offer a discount (often 2-5%) if you pay your premium once a year instead of monthly.
